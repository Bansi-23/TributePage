Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Iron Man of India

Sardar Patel dreamt of an independent nation and worked for it with full dedication.



Here's a time line of Sardar Patel life:


  • 1875 - Born in Nadiad, Gujarat

  • 1897 - Passed his high school and started preparing for law examination

  • 1910 - Went to pursue a degree in law and travelled to England

  • 1913 - Completed his law degree from Inns of Court

  • 1917 - Elected as the Secretary of the Gujarat Sabha, the Gujarat wing of the Indian National Congress

  • 1918 - led a massive "No Tax Campaign" that urged the farmers not to pay taxes after the British insisted on tax after the floods in Kaira

  • 1928 - led a "Bardoli Satyagraha" and its success gave rise to Patel becoming one of the main leaders of the independence movement

  • 1930 - Participating in the famous "Salt Satyagraha" movement initiated by Mahatma Gandhi

  • 1947 - Successful in integrating all of them barring Jammu and Kashmir, Junagarh and Hyderabad.

  • 1950 - Dies after age of 75

  • 1991 - He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honour.

  • 2014 - His birthday, October 31, was declared Rashtriya Ekta Divas.

  • 2018 - The Statue of Unity dedicated to Patel is unveiled as the world's tallest statue.
